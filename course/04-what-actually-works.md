# 4 · What actually works

After a graveyard that long, the obvious question is: *so is there any way to make money in markets, or
is it all hopeless?* The honest answer is neither "here's the secret" nor "give up." It's a
distinction most people never make.

## Making money ≠ beating the market

These are two completely different activities, and conflating them is why so many people lose:

- **Making money in stocks** is easy and reliable: own a broad, low-cost index fund and hold it for
  years. The equity risk premium is real — you're paid ~6–8%/yr on average for bearing the risk that
  stocks sometimes crash. In our own tests, plain buy-and-hold (Sharpe ≈ 0.65) *beat essentially every
  clever strategy.* Boring, slow, and it works.
- **Beating the market by trading** — timing in and out to do *better* than buy-and-hold — is what the
  whole graveyard was about. For mechanical, statistical rules on liquid assets and daily data, net of
  costs, it's arbitraged away. That's not pessimism; it's thirteen experiments and an exhaustive search.

The reliable thing you were looking for exists. It just isn't a *trade* — it's *ownership*.

## The three real edges (none of them is a secret formula)

Where does durable outperformance actually come from, for the rare people who achieve it? Never from a
hidden pattern in the price data. From one of these:

1. **Risk-premium harvesting.** Get *paid to bear risk* — the equity premium itself, or (with eyes
   open) carry and volatility premia. Real, accessible, but it's *compensation for risk, not alpha.* You
   will have bad years; that's what you're being paid for.
2. **Judgment.** Understanding a specific business better than the market — its moat, its cash flows,
   what it's worth. This is the one edge a diligent *individual* can genuinely have, because small and
   mid-caps are under-covered and you can be patient where funds can't. But it's *skill and years*, not
   a formula, and the base rates are humbling: ~85% of professional active managers underperform the
   index over 15 years.
3. **Behavior.** Not panic-selling in the crash. Not overtrading. A long horizon. This sounds trivial
   and is, in practice, the edge that most separates winners from losers — and it's the one genuinely
   available to everyone.

## The honest expectation

None of these makes you rich by Friday. Risk premia compound slowly. Judgment takes years to develop
and is easy to mistake for luck. Behavior is unglamorous. The people who *did* get rich quickly trading
are, overwhelmingly, the visible survivors of a process that quietly wiped out everyone who took the
same big swing — you hear about the lottery winner, not the ticket-buyers.

Your real edge over the professionals is not speed, data, or a secret signal. **It's patience and a
long time horizon** — you can hold through a drawdown that would force a leveraged fund to liquidate.
That's a genuine, durable advantage, and it costs nothing but discipline.

So: own the market cheaply, harvest the premium you're paid for bearing risk, do real homework if you
want a shot at more, and don't self-sabotage. Everything else in this course is there to stop you from
paying for the illusion that there's a shortcut.
